I'm excited to announce that my book on building production AI agents will be published in March 2026. This post gives a preview of the content and explains why I wrote it.

## Why This Book?

After building Shannon, a multi-agent platform deployed at several Fortune 500 companies, I realized there's a gap in the literature. Most books on AI agents focus on:

- Academic research and theoretical frameworks
- Simple tutorials that don't scale
- Specific frameworks (LangChain, AutoGPT) that change monthly

What's missing is practical guidance for building robust, production-grade agent systems that can handle real-world complexity.

## What the Book Covers

**Part I: Foundations**
- Agent architectures (ReAct, Plan-and-Solve, Reflexion)
- Tool use and function calling patterns
- Memory systems and context management

**Part II: Multi-Agent Systems**
- Communication patterns (hierarchical, flat, market-based)
- Coordination mechanisms (consensus, voting, auctions)
- Fault tolerance and recovery strategies

**Part III: Production Considerations**
- Observability and debugging
- Cost optimization
- Safety and alignment
- Regulatory compliance

**Part IV: Case Studies**
- Customer service automation
- Code generation systems
- Research assistants
- Trading agents

## Key Insights

One of the most important lessons from the book:

> **The hardest problems in agent systems are not AI problems — they're systems engineering problems.**

Retry logic, circuit breakers, idempotency, observability — these boring infrastructure concerns matter more than the latest prompting technique.

## Who Should Read This?

The book is written for:
- Software engineers building agent systems
- Technical leaders evaluating agent platforms
- Researchers wanting to understand production constraints

You should have basic familiarity with Python and LLMs, but no advanced math or ML background is required.
