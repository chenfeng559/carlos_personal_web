2025 was a pivotal year for AI. Let's review the major developments and look ahead to 2026.

## 2025: The Year of Agents

### 1. Agentic Workflows Went Mainstream

After years of research, autonomous AI agents finally became production-ready:

- **Coding agents**: Cursor, Claude Code, and GitHub Copilot moved from autocomplete to full-featured development partners
- **Research agents**: Systems that could autonomously search, synthesize, and report on complex topics
- **Enterprise automation**: Multi-agent systems handling customer service, HR, and operations

### 2. Reasoning Models Emerged

OpenAI's o1 and competitors introduced models that "think" before responding:

- Chain-of-thought reasoning became standard
- Test-time compute scaling showed promise
- Math and coding benchmarks saw dramatic improvements

### 3. Open Source Caught Up

Llama 3.1 and DeepSeek challenged closed-source dominance:

- 405B parameter models rivaling GPT-4
- Fully open weights enabling self-hosting
- Community fine-tunes exceeding base model performance

## What I Got Wrong

- **Multimodal dominance**: I expected image/video generation to be more transformative. Progress was steady but not revolutionary.
- **Robotics**: The crossover from simulation to real-world deployment was slower than anticipated.

## 2026 Predictions

### 1. The Great Consolidation

Dozens of AI startups will fail or be acquired. The infrastructure layer (compute, models) is consolidating around a few players. The application layer winners are emerging.

### 2. Regulatory Clarity (Finally)

The EU AI Act implementation will force standardization. US regulation will follow, focusing on:

- Transparency requirements for training data
- Mandatory safety evaluations for frontier models
- Liability frameworks for AI-caused harms

### 3. On-Device AI

Apple Silicon and Qualcomm chips will enable useful local LLMs. Privacy-conscious applications will shift from cloud to edge.

### 4. Scientific Discovery

AI systems will contribute meaningfully to:

- Drug discovery (first AI-designed drug in Phase 3 trials)
- Materials science (new superconductors or battery materials)
- Mathematics (automated theorem proving)

### 5. The Agent Economy

We'll see the first $1B+ companies with zero employees — fully automated businesses run by agent swarms handling everything from engineering to sales to support.
